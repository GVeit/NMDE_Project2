Grandma Rosie's Banana Bread

Home/Recipes/Sweet & Cakes

by: Grandma Rosie | 09.23.07 | Updated: 12.23.20

After my roommates and I bought some bananas and then proceeded to forget about them for about a week, banana bread was the route for us!

At the time, I didn't have the recipe, or any of Grandma Rosie's recipes for that matter, so I used a recipe that I found online...

What You'll Need:

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, cream the butter and sugar together. Soften the butter first, so that you can get a fairly even mixture.
  2. Dissolve 1 teaspoon of baking soda with 1 tablespoon of water.
  3. Beat in an egg, mixing thoroughly before adding in your baking soda/water mix.
  4. Next, add the vanilla extract and salt. Keep mixing!
  5. If you haven't yet mashed your bananas, do that now, and then add them into the mix along with however much cinnamon you think feels right. I generally put about a teaspoon in.
  6. Now that all the "wet" ingredients are mixed, add flour and mix well, until there isn't any evidence of flour in the bowl and everything has come together into a fairly thick batter.
  7. At this point, consider what you want to add to this banana bread. I recommend mixing in chocolate chips or walnuts, or even both, if you're feeling bold.
  8. Finally, pour the mixture into a loaf pan and bake it at 350 degrees for 50 minutes to an hour, or until a toothpick poked into the center of the loaf comes out clean.
